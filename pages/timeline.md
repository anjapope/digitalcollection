## Natural History Timeline

### 1. The rise of proboscideans in the Cenozoic

- Proboscideans became one of the most influential large herbivore groups of the Cenozoic.
- Their feeding and movement patterns helped open landscapes, spread grasses, and shape grazing ecosystems.
- In this sense, proboscideans were not just inhabitants of those environments; they were active makers of them.

### 2. The extinction of ancient proboscideans

- Ancient proboscidean extinction was driven partly by climate change, but largely by human hunting pressure.
- Multiple extinction events reduced their range and diversity over time.
- These losses culminated around 2000 BC, when the last mammoths disappeared on Wrangel Island.

### 3. Pressures on modern elephants

- **2000 BC:** Elephants still lived across a much wider range, including northern China, Mesopotamia, and northern Africa.
- **1600 AD:** The Atlantic ivory and slave trades intensified pressure on West African elephant populations.
- **1850–1900:** Zanzibar and East Africa became major centers of the ivory trade, further concentrating violence on elephant populations there.
