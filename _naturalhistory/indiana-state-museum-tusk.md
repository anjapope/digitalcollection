  This elephant tusk, held at the Indiana State Museum, is a striking reminder of the natural origins of ivory. Its size and form evoke the majesty of the animal it once belonged to, while its presence in the museum invites reflection on the intersections of science, history, and conservation. The tusk’s journey from living elephant to museum object tells a story of changing attitudes toward wildlife, collecting, and the value of natural heritage.
