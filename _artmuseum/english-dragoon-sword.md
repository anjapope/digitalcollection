  This English Dragoon sword, held at the Allen County Historical Society, features an ivory handle—a marker of status and global trade. The arrival of military goods adorned with ivory marks the first appearance of ivory in the region. This object embodies a dual process of colonization: externally, as goods and commerce generated from the exploitation of African resources fueled European expansion; and internally, as these same forces drove the displacement of indigenous peoples and the transformation of American landscapes. The sword stands as a witness to the entangled histories of violence, commerce, and cultural exchange that shaped the region.
